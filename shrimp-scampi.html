Shrimp Scampi


Here's a simple shrimp scampi recipe:

  • Yields: 4 servings
  • Prep time: 10 minutes
  • Cook time: 15 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine (such as Pinot Grigio)
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth (optional, for a richer sauce)
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Red pepper flakes (optional, for a little heat)
  • Cooked pasta or crusty bread, for serving
  • Equipment
  • Large skillet or pan
  • Small bowl

Instructions

  1. Get started:
  2. If using frozen shrimp, thaw them completely and pat them dry with paper towels.
  3. Season the shrimp with salt and pepper.
  4. Cook the shrimp:
  5. Melt the butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  6. Add the minced garlic and cook for about 30 seconds, until fragrant. Be careful not to let it burn.
  7. Add the shrimp to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until pink and cooked through. Remove the shrimp from the skillet and set aside.
  8. Make the sauce:
  9. Pour the white wine and chicken broth (if using) into the skillet. Bring to a simmer and cook for 2-3 minutes, until the sauce has reduced slightly.
  10. Stir in the lemon juice, parsley, and red pepper flakes (if using). Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  11. Return the shrimp to the skillet and toss to coat in the sauce.
  12. Serve:
  13. Serve the shrimp scampi immediately over cooked pasta or with crusty bread for dipping.
  14. Enjoy!

Tips and variations:

  • For a richer flavor, use a combination of butter and olive oil.
  • Add a pinch of red pepper flakes for a little heat.
  • Use different types of pasta, such as linguine, spaghetti, or angel hair.
  • Serve with a side of steamed vegetables, such as asparagus or broccoli.
  • Garnish with extra parsley and lemon wedges.
  • If you don't have white wine, you can substitute chicken broth or vegetable broth.
